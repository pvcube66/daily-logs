The logging industry can eradicate 30% of a rainforest where only a few commercially available trees were cut down. This rate can lead to clearing out of the entire rainforest in a few years. The destruction of rainforests is resulting in the loss of millions of species and impacts fertile land. Rainforests are responsible for a large portion of the world's oxygen supply. In conclusion, rainforests are an essential resource and contain many undiscovered possibilities. However, disruption through logging, colonization, and cattle ranching is leading to the loss of millions of species. It is essential that we take action to prevent further damage to our environment.

> The practice of damming rivers to create reservoirs has a long history, with examples dating back to Roman times. Over the years, the reasons for damming rivers have evolved, with the three main purposes being human consumption, irrigation, and industrial use. However, while damming provides benefits, it also causes significant problems, especially in underdeveloped countries. One of the primary advantages of damming rivers is to provide a reliable water source during droughts. Rainfall is never uniform enough to provide a steady flow of water, but reservoirs can hold water for extended periods which can be used during dry weather. Dams also help to control the flow of flood water in mountainous areas, which can destroy crops, homes, or animal and human life. Dams can also retain large quantities of water for controlled use. Another significant advantage of damming rivers is the production of hydroelectric power. By channeling water through conduits and using it as mechanical power for turbines, dams can produce vast amounts of energy. The reservoir water is also used in nuclear power stations for cooling. Dams can also provide a constant depth for rivers making them navigable by large vessels. On the downside, damming a river can cause problems. Great areas of land with trees, flora and fauna, farms and houses have to be inundated, and neither the residents who have resettled elsewhere nor environmentalists appreciate this. The problem is more acute when the countryside being spoiled is environmentally unique. Creating an artificial lake inevitably changes the character of a large area with farmland destroyed. In some people's opinion, moving can be inconvenient.
